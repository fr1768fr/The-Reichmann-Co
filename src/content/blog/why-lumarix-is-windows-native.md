Most new business software lives in a browser tab. Lumarix does not. We built it as a native Windows application, on purpose, and it is one of the decisions we are most sure about. Here is the thinking.

## Speed you can feel

Accounting is data entry. Real bookkeeping means hundreds of lines a day: invoices, receipts, journals, allocations. In a browser, many of those keystrokes make a round trip to a server somewhere, and you feel the lag. Multiply a small delay by a thousand entries and you have lost an afternoon.

Lumarix runs on your machine, at the speed of your hardware. There is no tab between you and your books, and no wait between a keystroke and a posting. A trial balance over a full year of transactions opens at once, because the data is right there and not three network hops away. For people who live in accounting software all day, that is not a nicety. It is the whole job feeling lighter.

## Your data stays yours

Every Lumarix company is a single file on your computer, a `.lmx` file you can see, copy, back up, or move to another machine. Your books are not sitting in someone else's cloud, under someone else's terms, one billing dispute away from being locked out.

For South African businesses this matters. Keeping client and financial data on your own machine and your own network is the simplest possible answer to a POPIA question, and it means you are never renting access to your own records. You own them, the way you own a filing cabinet.

## Built for how South Africa actually works

Connectivity here is not a given. Loadshedding, a dropped fibre line, a quiet day on mobile data: none of it should stop you from invoicing a customer or running a report. Because Lumarix and your data live on your machine, it keeps working when the internet does not. You only need to be online for the few things that genuinely need it, like activating your licence.

A local file is also a gift at year end. Handing your books to your accountant becomes sending a file, not exporting and re-importing and hoping nothing was lost along the way.

## The honest trade-offs

Native is a choice, not a free lunch, and we would rather be straight about it.

Lumarix is Windows-first today. A macOS build is on the roadmap, and until it lands, Mac users are the people we cannot serve yet. We are not happy about that, and we are working on it.

Native also means we ship updates to you rather than flipping a switch on a server. Lumarix handles that quietly, with built-in automatic updates, so staying current is not something you have to think about.

## A deliberate choice

The browser is a wonderful home for a lot of software. It is not the best home for the tool an accountant opens first thing every morning and closes last thing at night. That tool should be fast, it should respect that the data is yours, and it should not fall over when the line drops. On Windows, on your own machine, it can be all three.

That is why Lumarix is Windows-native. If you would like to put it through its paces, Lumarix is now in beta.
